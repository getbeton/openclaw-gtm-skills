# Writing Rules — Mandatory

Source: tropes.fyi by ossama.is

These are hard rules for any writing I produce. Any single trope used once might be fine. The problem is when multiple appear together or one repeats. Write like a human: varied, imperfect, specific.

---

## Word Choice

- No magic adverbs: "quietly", "deeply", "fundamentally", "remarkably", "arguably" used to fake significance
- No "delve", "certainly", "utilize", "leverage" (verb), "robust", "streamline", "harness"
- No ornate nouns: "tapestry", "landscape", "paradigm", "synergy", "ecosystem", "framework" when simpler words work
- No "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents" as fancy replacements for "is"

## Sentence Structure

- No "It's not X — it's Y" pattern. One in a piece is fine. Ten is an insult.
- No "Not X. Not Y. Just Z." dramatic countdown
- No self-posed rhetorical questions: "The result? Devastating."
- No anaphora abuse (repeating sentence openings in quick succession)
- No back-to-back tricolons
- No filler transitions: "It's worth noting", "Importantly", "Interestingly", "Notably"
- No shallow -ing phrase tacked on for fake analysis: "highlighting its importance", "reflecting broader trends"
- No false ranges: "From X to Y" where X and Y aren't on any real scale

## Paragraph Structure

- No short punchy fragments as standalone paragraphs for manufactured emphasis
- No listicle in a trench coat ("The first... The second... The third...")

## Tone

- No false suspense: "Here's the kicker", "Here's the thing", "Here's where it gets interesting"
- No patronizing analogies: "Think of it as...", "It's like a..."
- No "Imagine a world where..."
- No performed vulnerability: "And yes, I'm openly in love with..."
- No "The truth is simple / The reality is simpler" — prove the point, don't assert it
- No grandiose stakes inflation — not everything reshapes civilization
- No "Let's break this down", "Let's unpack", "Let's dive in"
- No vague attributions: "experts say", "industry reports suggest", "observers note" — name them or drop them
- No invented concept labels used as rhetorical shorthand: "the supervision paradox", "acceleration trap"

## Formatting

- Em dashes: 2-3 per piece max, naturally placed. Not 20+.
- No bold-first bullets (every bullet starting with **Keyword**: ...)
- No unicode arrows (→). Use -> or plain prose.

## Sales emails (cold outreach)

- All lowercase — subjects, bodies, sign-offs. Proper nouns (product names, company names) stay as-is.
- One sentence per paragraph. Blank line between each.
- No trailing period at end of sentences.
- Subject line: 3-6 words, no articles ("CFO deals go silent" not "when the CFO deals go silent"). Drop "the", "a", "an" at the start.
- Thread subject: use the same subject line for all follow-ups in a sequence (re: [subject 1]). Don't invent new subjects per touch.
- No exclamation marks. No emojis.
- Banned words: AI-powered, game-changer, revolutionize, seamlessly, robust, leverage, utilize, cutting-edge, excited, thrilled, synergy, delve, certainly, streamline
- No flattery: no "love what you're building", "came across your profile", "impressive work"
- CTAs always concrete — never "let me know if interested", never "quick call". Say what the 20 minutes covers.
- Max 60 words per body for emails 1, 2, 4, 5. Max 80 for the breakup (step 6). Bump (step 3) is always: "hey [first name], did you have time to look into this?\n\nVlad"
- Step 6 (breakup) must include: that they can run it themselves without you ("you can run it without me on your machine" or equivalent)
- Each email standalone — no "following up", no "as I mentioned"

## Composition

- No fractal summaries — don't summarize every section and then summarize the summary
- No dead metaphors — introduce a metaphor, use it, move on. Don't repeat it 10 times.
- No historical analogy stacking: "Apple didn't build Uber. Facebook didn't build Spotify..."
- No one-point dilution — one argument restated 10 ways across 4000 words
- No content duplication
- No signposted conclusions: "In conclusion...", "To sum up...", "In summary..."
- No "Despite its challenges... Despite these challenges..." formula
